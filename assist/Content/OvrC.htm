Creating a Room

Rooms allows you to group devices for better organization and efficient navigation. You can create rooms for different building floors, business departments, or other spaces on-site, making it easier to find and manage specific devices and provide a better overview of the device layout on-site.

This feature allows a one-way sync between the OvrC location and the Control4 project. Creating rooms in OvrC will automatically create the room in Composer, saving you time; however, rooms that are added in Composer are not synchronized with OvrC at this time.

Follow the steps below to create a room.

  1. Select a customer then the Rooms tab.

By default, OvrC places all devices under Unassigned. Use this room for devices that regularly leave the network and don’t need to be monitored.

  1. Click CREATE ROOM to add a new room to the site.

OvrC makes suggestions as you type to provide quick and accurate entries.

  1. Press Enter to add the room.

Adding Devices to a Room

You can a device to a room from the Rooms tab or the Devices tab. Follow the steps below to add a device to a room from the Rooms tab.

  1. From the Rooms tab, click ADD DEVICES TO ROOM.

This opens a new window with search and auto-fill functionality.

  1. Either start typing the name of the device you’d like to add or click the expansion icon to see a list of unassigned devices.

  1. Click ADD DEVICES when finished.

Follow the steps below to add devices to a room from the Devices tab.

  1. Select the DEVICES tab.

  1. Click the checkboxes to the left of the appropriate devices, then click the Move to Rooms icon.

A new window appears giving a summary of the selected devices.

  1. Type the name of the room to search for it or select a room from the drop-down list.

  1. Click CONTINUEto move the devices.

Pro Tip: If you’re organizing an existing project into rooms, it may be easier to select devices from the device list first, then create the room using this method. Try it and see if it improves your workflow.

 

Naming Devices

Follow the steps below to rename a device so it’s easy to identify.

  1. Select a Customer, then the DEVICES tab.

  1. Click the device name you want to edit.

  1. Click the three dots on the right-side of the page and click Edit Name.

  1. Type the new device name and then click SAVE.

 

Pro Tip: Use a clear name for each device, including the location in the device name when applicable.

 

Naming Network Device Ports

As you configure network devices, it’s helpful to name based on the connected device. Follow the steps below to rename network ports so they’re easy to identify. In this example, we’ll rename the ports of the switch.

  1. Select a customer then the DEVICES tab.

  1. Click the network device name.

  1. Click the information icon associated with the port you want to rename.

  1. Type the name of the device connected to the port in the Port Name field.

  1. Click SAVE and repeat this process for all other ports and network devices.

 

Naming Power Supply (WattBox) Outlets

As you configure the power supply (i.e., WattBox), it’s helpful to name the outlets based on the device it powers. Follow the steps below to rename power supply outlets so they’re easy to identify.

  1. Select a customer then the DEVICES tab.

  1. Click the power supply device name.

  1. Click the CONFIGURE tab for the device.

  1. In General Settings, click Outlet Options.

  1. Type the name of the device connected to the outlet in the Outlet Name field. Repeat this process for all outlets.

  1. Click SAVE.

 

Enabling Notifications

Follow the steps below to enable notification in OvrC.

  1. Click User from the left-side menu.

  1. In Global Notification Settings, toggle on Enable Notifications.

  1. Toggle on the specific notifications you want to receive.

  1. Click SAVE.

 

Registering a Controller

Before registering a controller through OvrC, you must verify your account and connect it to your Control4 project. Follow the steps below to verify your OvrC account.

  1. Click ACCOUNT.

  2. Click VERIFY ACCOUNT.

  1. Enter your dealer login credentials.

  2. Click Verify Control4 Account.

Once your dealer account is verified, follow the steps below to register a controller in OvrC.

  1. Select a Customer, then the DEVICES tab.

  1. Click SCAN to automatically claim the newly added controller. (Note: By default, OvrC scans the network hourly, so the device may already be claimed and listed.

If the device list is empty, click ADD DEVICE to manually enter the MAC address and Service Tag number.

  1. Click CATEGORIES.

  2. Click Start New Project.

  3. Click Control4 Project.

  4. Select the controller. (Note: Only one primary controller can be added per location.)

  5. Click Set up and Register.

Once a controller is registered, temporary credentials are made available in OvrC for testing with the mobile app prior to handing the system to the customer.

 

Verifying and Editing Customer Information Before Hand-Off

Follow the steps below to verify the customer’s information is accurate in OvrC.

  1. Click List on the left-side menu.

  2. Click the customer.

  3. Review the customer’s information for accuracy.

    • If the customer’s physical address is inaccurate, click the three dots in the upper, right-hand corner of the page and click Edit Location.

  • If the customer’s name, phone number, or email address is inaccurate, click the three dots in the upper, right-hand corner of the page and click Edit Customer.

  1. Make your changes and click Save.

Handing Off the Project to the Customer

The following steps detail the customer hand-off process that an integrator completes when ready to provide the customer access to their OvrC account.

To get started, follow the steps below to invite the customer to their project via OvrC.

  1. Click List from the left-side menu and click the customer.

  2. Click the Settings tab.

  3. Click Invite User.

  1. Enter the customer’s email address in the Email Address field.

  1. Select the Control4 and OvrC Connect checkboxes.

  1. Click SEND INVITE.

The customer receives an email from Control4 with a one-time use passcode to log into their OvrC account.

The invitation will expire after five days if not accepted, and a new invite must be generated.

 

 

The remainder of the customer hand-off process is completed within the OvrC Connect app. Ensure the customer completes the following steps to successfully log into their OvrC account.

  1. The customer downloads the OvrC Connect app from Apple App Store or Google Play Store and clicks on Register Your System.

 

  1. The customer reviews and agrees to the Control4 Terms & Privacy Policy.

 

  1. The customer creates their account by entering the one-time use passcode, their email address, and a new password. 

 

  1. The customer reviews, confirms, or edits their information.

 

  1. The customer reviews information about the required Connect annual plan and selects Control4 Assist, Assist Premium, or None to opt out of a Control4 provided Support plan.

 

  1. The customer views additional details about Connect and Assist plans.

 

  1. The customer reviews their selected plan summary.

 

  1. The customer selects their preferred method of payment and enters their payment information.

 

  1. The customer completes the registration process and clicks Start Your Journey.

The customer is brought to the main screen of their mobile app experience.
